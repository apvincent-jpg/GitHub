Rock Climbing Holds

Scroll to learn about different types of rock climbing holds!

Pockets

What are they?

Pocket holds are climbing holds with small holes or dips where climbers insert one, two, or three fingers to grip. They rely heavily on finger strength because you can’t use your whole hand like on bigger holds.

What I think!

Pockets are not my favorite holds. They require a lot of finger strength and use of friction to stay up. Although, I do like deep pockets for pulling myself up.

Jugs

What are they?

Jug holds are large climbing holds that are easy to grip because they have a deep, secure edge you can wrap your whole hand around. They allow climbers to hold on comfortably without needing much finger strength.

What I think!

Jug holds are great! They are very beginner friendly, and always a treat when placed in the middle of a difficult climb.

Side Pulls

What are they?

Side pulls are climbing holds that are gripped from the side instead of from above. Climbers pull on them sideways, using body position and tension to stay balanced.

What I think!

I am a big fan of side pulls! I like them because, unlike other difficult holds that require a lot of arm strength, side pulls are all about positioning your body, and balancing your weight while leaning to one side. Moreover, when multiple side pulls are available, you can pull in or out to keep yourself up -- which is another fun challenge!

Footholds

What are they?

Footholds are small climbing holds designed specifically for placing your feet on rather than your hands. They are usually small and require precise foot placement and balance to use effectively.

What I think!

Footholds are what you put your feet on in most climbs, so it is kind of hard to dislike them. Although, if a foothold is too small or I am not paying enough attention to my foot placement, it is easy to fall. Falling quickly off a foothold is pretty scary, however, if I place my feet correctly, they can be very helpful.

Crimps

What are they?

This hold is a small edge you grab onto with your fingertips. It is a more difficult hold, since your fingers are bent sharply and that is the only muscle holding you up.

What I think!

Controversially... I love crimps! Crimps rely on strong use of many fingers (usually) bent around a hold. I like these hold because I find it really easy to straighten my arms and hold all my weight in my lower body -- giving my arms a rest.
