Rock Climbing Holds

Learn about different types of rock climbing holds!

Pockets

Pocket holds are climbing holds with small holes or dips where climbers insert one, two, or three fingers to grip. They rely heavily on finger strength because you can’t use your whole hand like on bigger holds. Pockets can vary in depth and size, with deeper pockets being easier and shallow ones more difficult. They are common on indoor climbing walls and outdoor limestone routes.

Jugs

Jug holds are large climbing holds that are easy to grip because they have a deep, secure edge you can wrap your whole hand around. They allow climbers to hold on comfortably without needing much finger strength. Jugs are often used on beginner routes and for resting during climbs because they provide a very stable and safe grip.

Side Pulls

Side pulls are climbing holds that are gripped from the side instead of from above. Climbers pull on them sideways, using body position and tension to stay balanced. They often require leaning your body in the opposite direction of the hold to maintain grip. Side pulls help develop technique and body control rather than just strength.

Footholds

Footholds are small climbing holds designed specifically for placing your feet on rather than your hands. They are usually small and require precise foot placement and balance to use effectively. Footholds help support your body weight and allow you to push upward with your legs, which is more efficient than pulling with your arms.

Crimp

This hold is a small edge you grab onto with your fingertips. It is a more difficult hold, since your fingers are bent sharply and that is the only muscle holding you up.
